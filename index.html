EDUCATIONAL SYSTEM IN THE PHILIPPINES

The Philippine education system encompasses all levels of education from pre-school to tertiary education, including vocational and technical education. This includes three levels of basic education, which are pre-school, primary, and secondary education, as well as tertiary education, which includes university education.

Pre-school education is not mandatory but is provided for children under the age of six. It is meant to prepare children for primary education by providing them with socialization and fundamental skills such as language and math.

Primary education refers to the first six years of basic education and is compulsory for children aged 6 to 12. The primary level covers basic literacy and numeracy skills, as well as the basic concepts of natural science, social science, Filipino and values education, arts, music, and physical education.

Secondary education is the second level of basic education and is compulsory for children aged 12 to 15. This level covers more advanced academic subjects, as well as the continuation of character development, social studies, and values education.

Tertiary education refers to college or university education and provides students with specialized knowledge and skills in a particular field. This level includes undergraduate and graduate degrees, as well as vocational and technical education.

Vocational and technical education is a type of tertiary education that focuses on developing practical skills for specific occupations, such as engineering, architecture, and nursing.
